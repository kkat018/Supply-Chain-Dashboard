This supply chain analytics initiative delivers an end-to-end Control Tower view of global logistics performance by consolidating revenue, fulfillment, and delivery execution data into a single analytical framework. The dashboard translates high-volume transactional data into decision-ready insights by directly linking operational reliability (on-time delivery, shipping delays) with financial outcomes (revenue and order profitability). This integrated perspective enables leadership to identify not only where failures occur, but why they materially impact customer experience and margin performance.

## Executive Summary: Global Logistics Performance
The organization currently generates $12.77M in total revenue, supported by a broad, high-volume order base across multiple shipping tiers and product categories. Despite this strong top-line performance, fulfillment execution presents a critical risk.

* The On-Time Delivery Rate is only 17.8%, meaning more than four out of five orders are delivered later than promised.
* The average shipping delay is 3.5 days, a duration that materially exceeds customer expectations and increases the likelihood of dissatisfaction, returns, and lost repeat business.
* Late delivery risk across the network averages 54.8%, indicating systemic rather than isolated operational issues.

Collectively, these indicators show a clear disconnect between revenue generation and fulfillment reliability, positioning logistics performance as a primary constraint on both customer satisfaction and long-term profitability. 

## SupplyChainDashboard

### Page 1: Logistics Executive Overview
This page provides a strategic, macro-level view of how shipping tier mix and product category demand shape both logistics workload and financial outcomes.

#### Shipping Tier Dependency
Order volume is heavily concentrated in Standard Class shipping, which accounts for 59.81% (39K orders) of all shipments. Second Class and First Class together contribute approximately 34.8%, while Same Day shipping represents only 5.43% (4K orders). This distribution indicates that the organization prioritizes cost efficiency and scale, but also exposes the business to higher late-delivery risk associated with lower-priority shipping modes. 

#### Demand Concentration by Product Category
The Top 5 categories by order volume—including Baseball & Softball, Tennis & Racquet, Lacrosse, Fitness Accessories, and Strength Training—drive the majority of logistics demand. These categories create sustained fulfillment pressure, particularly when inventory is not optimally positioned relative to demand regions. High-volume categories therefore amplify the operational impact of even modest delivery delays. 

#### Profitability vs. Logistics Performance
The profitability scatter analysis demonstrates a strong relationship between delivery speed and financial performance. While most categories cluster around the 3.5-day average delay, categories with shorter shipping delays (e.g., Computers at ~3.1 days) consistently generate higher average profit per order. This confirms that logistics reliability is not merely a cost center metric but a direct contributor to margin optimization. 

### Page 2: Operational Root Cause Analysis
This page leverages AI-driven diagnostics, funnels, and heatmaps to isolate the root causes behind poor on-time performance.

#### Late Delivery Risk Decomposition
The AI decomposition tree identifies Shipping Mode as the dominant risk driver. First Class shipping exhibits a 93.3% late delivery risk, contradicting customer expectations for premium service. Even when segmented by region and category, this risk remains consistently elevated. For example, Eastern Asia shipments of Women’s Clothing show a 58.2% late delivery rate, indicating that geographic complexity compounds mode-level inefficiencies rather than replacing them. 

#### On-Time Performance Efficiency
The funnel analysis highlights a stark performance gap across shipping tiers:
* Same Day shipping achieves ~49.3% on-time efficiency, making it the most reliable option.
* Second Class and Standard Class both drop to ~19% efficiency, despite accounting for the majority of total order volume.
This reveals that operational processes are not scaling effectively across lower-cost tiers, resulting in widespread service degradation. 

#### Delivery Risk by Customer Segment
The delivery risk heatmap confirms that these issues are systemic across customer types. First Class shipping exceeds 95% late delivery risk for Consumer, Corporate, and Home Office segments alike. This uniformity eliminates customer mix as a primary driver and points instead to carrier performance, SLA misalignment, or internal fulfillment bottlenecks. 


## Strategic Recommendations

Grounded directly in the dashboard insights, the following actions are recommended:

#### Carrier and SLA Renegotiation
With First Class shipping failing over 95% of the time, immediate action is required. Leadership should renegotiate carrier SLAs, introduce penalty clauses tied to on-time performance, or temporarily remove this tier from customer options until reliability improves.

#### Region-Focused Process Optimization
Persistent late-delivery patterns in Eastern Asia and the US Center indicate structural inefficiencies. Targeted interventions—such as regional carrier diversification or fulfillment process redesign—should be prioritized regardless of product category.

#### Inventory Re-Positioning for High-Volume Categories
High-demand categories like Strength Training, currently experiencing ~3.8-day delays, would benefit from forward inventory staging closer to demand centers. Reducing real shipping time in these categories offers immediate gains in both on-time delivery and per-order profitability.
